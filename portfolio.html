(Fish) Brain Computer Interface

For 11 weeks at Backyard Brains, I developed a neuroethological research tool for weakly-electric fish. These fish emit sinusoidal bioelectric signals for navigation and communication. This made it easy to mimic and manipulate the neural circuit that controls the frequency of their signals.

BrainTense - 5C Hackathon S2016

Brain + Muscles = BrainTense.

How does it work?

We built an EMG circuit and hacked an EEG headset to convert the brain and body's electrical activity into digital output. In turn, this data was used to control a sprite in a game. This simple demonstration provides some insight to applications of neurotechnology in various avenues like gaming.
